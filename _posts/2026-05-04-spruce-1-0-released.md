We're delighted to announce the release of [SPRUCE 1.0](https://opensourcegreenops.cloud/){:target='_blank'} — an important milestone for the project and, we hope, for everyone trying to get a credible picture of their cloud carbon footprint.

## Why 1.0 matters

A `1.0` is more than a version number. It's a commitment: that the methodology has settled, that the interfaces are stable enough to build on, and that the project is ready to be used in earnest — not just experimented with. Reaching it took longer than we'd have liked, but that's the point. The numbers SPRUCE produces feed into decisions about architecture, procurement and reporting; they have to be defensible.

## Why SPRUCE exists

Measuring the environmental impact of cloud services is genuinely hard. Providers offer data that is *incomplete*, *delayed*, or aggregated in ways that make it impossible to act on. You can't optimise what you can't see, and you can't trust a number whose methodology you can't inspect.

SPRUCE takes a different approach: it is *open source*, the methodology is *transparent*, and the estimates can be reproduced and challenged. That matters because nobody — not us, not the hyperscalers, not the standards bodies — has all the answers yet. The honest thing is to put the working out in public and let it be improved.

## What's next

1.0 is a foundation, not a finish line. We have a roadmap of improvements ahead: broader service coverage, better handling of regional grid intensity, and tighter integration with the FinOps tooling our clients already use. The full release notes and documentation live on the [project site](https://opensourcegreenops.cloud/){:target='_blank'}.

If you're using SPRUCE — or want to — we'd love to hear from you. Issues, pull requests and awkward questions are all welcome on [GitHub](https://github.com/digitalpebble){:target='_blank'}, and you can always [get in touch directly](mailto:contact@digitalpebble.com).
